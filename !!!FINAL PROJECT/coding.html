Hackerman

Back in Middle School, my mom and dad gifted me with a year-long plan with Youth Digital which allowed me to create my own Minecraft mod. This was huge for me, even if I had no clue what kind of mod I wanted to make. I took up making the mod and finished in less than 2-months, but unfortunately that program is lost to a failed hard drive and a now-dead service; but since then, my passion for programming has lived on.

I had to wait until High School, but as soon as I was able to I signed up for Computer Science and began learning the basics of programming . At this point, I had already learned some basic coding principals from both my middle school Minecraft mod and personal experimentation with a Discord bot. I was excited to take the next step and getting official guidance on how to program, along with the tools to learn new programming languages.

During my final year of High School, learning essentially stopped after Spring Break due to COVID. It was during this break, however, that I invested time in my own personal projects. I worked on my Discord Bot, Johnathan Dazzle, and even made and sold the code to an esports org to assist them in running tournaments. Dazzle was originally written in plain JavaScript using Node.JS, but I've since refactored him to be written in TypeScript and compiled to JavaScript. I'm very happy with how Dazzle has turned out, and you can find a link to his GitHub repository at the right. He's a fairly rudimentary bot meant to assist in playing TTRPGs, but I'm very proud with how everything looks and works. I even self-host it, but it tends to go down after an extended period for unknown reasons (I have it daemonized on my raspberry pi, which is always powered and connected to the internet).
